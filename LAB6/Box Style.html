Historical Places in the Philippines

Rizal Park (Luneta)

Rizal Park in Manila is a historic site dedicated to the country’s national hero, Dr. José Rizal. It is the location where he was executed in 1896, sparking greater unity among Filipinos in their fight for independence. Today, it is a place of reflection, leisure, and national pride.

Intramuros

Intramuros, also known as the "Walled City," is the oldest district in Manila. Built during the Spanish colonial period, it served as the center of politics, religion, and education. Its preserved walls, churches, and fortresses remain a symbol of the Philippines’ rich colonial history.

Banaue Rice Terraces

The Banaue Rice Terraces, often called the "Eighth Wonder of the World," were carved into the mountains of Ifugao by the ancestors of the indigenous people over 2,000 years ago. These terraces showcase Filipino ingenuity and sustainable farming practices that continue to this day.
